# Deleting Your Account

This guide walks you through the process of deleting your account and explains what happens to your data.

## Before You Delete

<Warning>
Account deletion is permanent and cannot be undone. Please make sure you've backed up any important data before proceeding.
</Warning>

Before deleting your account, consider the following:

- **Export your data**: Download any important information you want to keep
- **Cancel subscriptions**: Ensure all active subscriptions are cancelled to avoid future charges
- **Revoke API keys**: Invalidate any API keys associated with your account
- **Notify team members**: If you're part of a team, inform relevant members about your departure

## How to Delete Your Account

### Via the Dashboard

<Steps>
  <Step title="Navigate to Settings">
    Log in to your account and go to **Settings** → **Account**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Scroll to Danger Zone">
    At the bottom of the page, find the **Danger Zone** section.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click Delete Account">
    Click the **Delete Account** button.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm Deletion">
    Enter your password and type your username to confirm. Click **Permanently Delete** to proceed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Via API

You can also delete your account programmatically using our API:

```bash
curl -X DELETE https://api.example.com/v1/account \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{"confirm": true}'
```

```python
import requests

response = requests.delete(
    "https://api.example.com/v1/account",
    headers={
        "Authorization": "Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN",
        "Content-Type": "application/json"
    },
    json={"confirm": True}
)

if response.status_code == 200:
    print("Account deleted successfully")
```

```javascript
const response = await fetch('https://api.example.com/v1/account', {
  method: 'DELETE',
  headers: {
    'Authorization': 'Bearer YOUR_API_TOKEN',
    'Content-Type': 'application/json'
  },
  body: JSON.stringify({ confirm: true })
});

if (response.ok) {
  console.log('Account deleted successfully');
}
```

## What Happens to Your Data

When you delete your account, the following occurs:

| Data Type | Retention Period | Action |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| Profile information | Immediate | Permanently deleted |
| API keys | Immediate | Revoked and deleted |
| Usage logs | 30 days | Anonymized then deleted |
| Billing history | 7 years | Retained for legal compliance |
| Uploaded files | 30 days | Permanently deleted |

<Note>
Some data may be retained for legal and compliance purposes as required by applicable laws.
</Note>

## Grace Period

After initiating account deletion, you have a **14-day grace period** during which:

- Your account is deactivated but not deleted
- You can log in to cancel the deletion request
- Your data remains intact
- API access is disabled

To cancel deletion during the grace period:

1. Log in with your credentials
2. You'll see a banner asking if you want to restore your account
3. Click **Cancel Deletion** to restore full access

## Frequently Asked Questions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I recover my account after deletion?">
    No, once the 14-day grace period has passed and your account is permanently deleted, it cannot be recovered. Make sure to back up any data you need before deleting.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="What happens to my team's data if I'm the owner?">
    If you're a team owner, you must transfer ownership to another member before deleting your account, or the entire team workspace will be deleted along with your account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Will I get a refund for unused subscription time?">
    Refunds for unused subscription time are handled on a case-by-case basis. Please contact support before deleting your account to discuss refund options.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I create a new account with the same email?">
    Yes, after your account is permanently deleted (after the grace period), you can create a new account using the same email address.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
